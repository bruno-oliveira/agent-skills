# The Coffee Method

> A short, opinionated guide — and a demonstration of what this template makes of ordinary Markdown.

## Why Method Matters

Good coffee is not luck; it is a small number of variables held steady. Grind size, water temperature, ratio, and time — control these and the cup follows. This section is prose: notice the comfortable measure, the line height, and the section rule above.

Everything here is plain Markdown. The template supplies the typography.

## The Four Variables

The table below renders with uppercase mono headers and an emphasized first column — ideal for reference and decision tables.

| Variable | Target | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Grind | Medium-fine | Controls extraction rate; too fine over-extracts (bitter) |
| Water | 94–96°C | Hot enough to extract, not to scorch |
| Ratio | 1:16 | Grams of coffee to grams of water; the strength dial |
| Time | 3–4 min | The contact window; longer pulls more, and eventually too much |

## A Callout

> The single highest-leverage change most people can make is buying a burr grinder. Consistent particle size does more for the cup than any other upgrade.

## The Method, Condensed

1. Weigh your beans (start at 1:16).
2. Grind just before brewing.
3. Pour at 94–96°C.
4. Taste. Change **one** variable. Repeat.

---

Iterate one variable at a time and you converge on your cup in a week. Change everything at once and you learn nothing — the same discipline that governs any good experiment.
